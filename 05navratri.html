Tales of Tradition

Long ago, a demon king named Mahishasura was causing chaos on Earth. Despite his great strength, he could not defeat the Goddess Durga, who fought him for nine days and nights. On the tenth day, Vijayadashami, she triumphed, symbolizing the victory of good over evil. Every year, Navratri is celebrated to honor Goddess Durga’s strength and victory. For nine days, people worship her in different forms, fasting, dancing, and offering prayers. The final day is marked by grand celebrations, dances, and feasts, celebrating the power of feminine strength.

Festive Flavours

During Navratri, people enjoy vrat-friendly foods like sabudana khichdi, kuttu ki puri, and singhara halwa. Fresh fruits, nuts, and drinks like lassi or coconut water are also popular.

Ethnic Elegance

During Navratri, women wear vibrant lehenga cholis, sarees, and chaniya cholis, often embellished with mirrors and embroidery. Men wear kurta-pajamas or dhoti with traditional accessories for the festive dances.
