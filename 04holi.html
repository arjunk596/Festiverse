Tales of Tradition

Long ago, there was a demoness named Holika, who was immune to fire. She tricked the young devotee Prahlad, who was a follower of Lord Vishnu, into sitting on a pyre with her. But due to her evil intentions, she was burned in the flames, while Prahlad remained unharmed, saved by Lord Vishnu. To celebrate Prahlad’s victory over evil, people began the tradition of Holi, a festival of colors. On this day, people throw colored powders at each other, dance, sing, and celebrate the arrival of spring and the joy of life. Holi reminds everyone to rejoice in life, forget differences, and embrace unity.

Festive Flavours

During Holi, traditional foods like gujiya, malpua, dahi vada, and puran poli are enjoyed. Savory snacks like papad and refreshing drinks like thandai with festive spices add to the celebrations.

Ethnic Elegance

During Holi, people wear white or light-colored clothes to highlight the vibrant colors of the festival. Traditional attire like kurta-pajamas for men and sarees or salwar kameez for women are popular.
