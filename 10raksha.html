Tales of Tradition

Long ago, the Hindu god Indra was in battle with demons. His wife, Sachi, worried for his safety, tied a sacred thread around his wrist for protection. This act of love and care grew into the tradition of Raksha Bandhan—a bond of protection between brothers and sisters. On this day, sisters tie a rakhi (sacred thread) around their brothers’ wrists, symbolizing protection, while brothers promise to safeguard their sisters. The festival strengthens familial bonds and celebrates love, care, and unity.

Festive Flavours

During Raksha Bandhan, sweets like rasgulla, kaju katli, and barfi are commonly enjoyed. Puran poli, seviyan, and sweets made of coconut are also prepared to celebrate the bond between siblings.

Ethnic Elegance

During Raksha Bandhan, people wear festive attire like kurta-pajamas or sherwanis for men, and sarees, lehenga cholis, or salwar kameez for women. Bright colors and traditional patterns are popular choices.
