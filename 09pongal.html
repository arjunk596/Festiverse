Tales of Tradition

In the state of Tamil Nadu, there was a time when the people faced a long period of famine. They prayed to the Sun God for good harvests. After a year of waiting, the first harvest finally came, bringing prosperity and joy. To thank the Sun God, people celebrated by cooking a special dish of freshly harvested rice, known as Pongal. Every year, Pongal is celebrated with a grand feast, cooking the dish in a pot, offering prayers, and expressing gratitude for a good harvest. It’s a time for family, friends, and community to come together and celebrate the bounty of the earth.

Festive Flavours

During Pongal, traditional foods like ven pongal, sakkarai pongal, vadai, and sambar are prepared. Sweet pongal, made with rice, jaggery, and coconut, is a highlight of the celebration.

Ethnic Elegance

During Navratri, women wear vibrant lehenga cholis, sarees, and chaniya cholis, often embellished with mirrors and embroidery. Men wear kurta-pajamas or dhoti with traditional accessories for the festive dances.
